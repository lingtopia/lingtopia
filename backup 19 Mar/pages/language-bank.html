Basic Sentence Structure

Lingua Nova follows a Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) word order, similar to English.

Mi parla lingua. = I speak the language.

Noun Endings

All nouns end in -a (singular) or -as (plural).

lingua = language

linguas = languages

Adjective Placement

Adjectives typically come before the noun they modify and end in -i.

novi lingua = new language

boni amiku = good friend

Verb Conjugation

Verbs are conjugated by adding suffixes to the verb stem:

Tense Suffix Example
Present -a parla (speak)
Past -av parlav (spoke)
Future -ar parlar (will speak)
Conditional -ari parlari (would speak)

Pronouns

Personal pronouns in Lingua Nova:

Person Singular Plural
1st Person mi (I) nos (we)
2nd Person tu (you) vos (you all)
3rd Person il/el/ol (he/she/it) ils (they)
